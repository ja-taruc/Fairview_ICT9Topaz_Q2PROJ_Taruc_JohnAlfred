How to make patupat

Patupat is a snack which is made of glutinous rice which is often sold on the streets of Bontoc, Mountain Province, it also known as Suman Ilocano

Preparation time

  • Total: 3 Hours
  • Preparation: 20 minutes
  • Cooking: 2 hours and 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups of Glutinous Rice
  • 1 Large Can of Coconut Milk
  • 1 Teaspoon of Salt
  • Clean Bananaleaves, cut into 6x12 inch pieces

Instructions

  1. Prepare Rice Wash rice with water and drain. Soak rice in water for 1 hour and drain after.
  2. Cook Rice In a pan, pour in coconut milk, salt, glutinous rice, sugar(optional)and let it cook over low heat. Keep stirring until all the coconut milk is absorbed by the rice. Turn off the heat once the rice is half cooked and let it cool.
  3. Form the suman Swirl the banana leaves in your palm to form a cone shape leaf. Scoop a ½ cup or less of cooked glutinous rice then seal it on top with a toothpick or plastic strings.
  4. Boil the suman cones Put the suman into a pot and fill it with water. Bring it to a boil and let it simmer. After that set it aside to cool down
  5. Enjoy: Serve it cooled down, recommended to eat it with Barako Coffee
