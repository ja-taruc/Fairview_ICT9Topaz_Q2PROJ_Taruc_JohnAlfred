How to cook Pinikpikan

A dish which originates from the mountains of Cordillera and is specially prepared by beating a live chicken with a stick and a elderly inspecting it's organs specifically lungs and liver which determines the future of the people who will eat it.

Preparation time

  • Total: 1 Hour and 20 Minutes
  • Preparation: 20 minutes
  • Cooking: 1 Hour

Ingredients

  • 1 Whole Native Chicken
  • 1 Big Bunch of Bok Choy
  • 2 Sayote, Sliced
  • 2 Stalks of celery, sliced
    • 1 Inch Piece of Ginger, thinly sliced
  • 2 Red Onions, chopped
  • 4 Cloves of Garlic, smashed
  • 1/2 Cup of Tapuy or any rice wine
  • Water
  • Oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • Optional: Etag or any fatty pork

Instructions

  1. Cut chicken in half: Cut chicken in half, season with salt and then let it grill for 15 minutes each side skin side up.
  2. Cut chicken into pieces After grilling, let the chicken cool down then cut into several pieces
  3. Making of stock Add oil, into a pot then saute the celery, ginger, onion and garlic.
  4. Adding of Liquids Add in the chicken, rice wine, and water, then pour enoughw water until it covers the chicken. Bring to a boil and let it simmer in medium low heat for 30 minutes
  5. Add sayota Add sayote and bok choy, then let it simmer for 8 more minutes.
  6. Enjoy: Serve into a bowl and enjoy it while it is hot.
